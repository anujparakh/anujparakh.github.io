The Phantom Guitar

The Phantom Guitar is the perfect mix of two of my childhood hobbies: playing guitar, and arduinos/embedded development. I eventually had the great idea to bring both of my hobbies together. I found the LightBlue Bean by PunchThrough (Click on the picture to learn more) which was a perfect fit for my project.

The LightBlue Bean is a BLE device and works much like an Arduino, except over Bluetooth Low Energy. It has an LED, accelerometer and numerous I/O pins.
I wrote iOS and MacOS apps where the user can select guitar chords to play, and then use the Bean like a guitar pick, strumming in air while getting the feeling and sound of playing a guitar.

Ultimate Tic Tac Toe

When I first learnt iOS and Android programming, I decided to make the simple but fun game of Tic-Tac-Toe. Then, I found out about Ultimate Tic Tac Toe, a much harder version of the same game (to try the game, click on the picture).

Playing the usual small Tic-Tac-Toe is much easier than playing the same game on 9 boards with a bunch of new rules. It was also a challenge to code it, but I enjoyed the challenge. This project helped me understand how important Object Oriented Programming is, because I was able to create one single board and make 9 of the same boards, making my task much easier.

Traffic Light Controller

As part of a college class, I have started working with Raspberry Pis. I have worked extensively with Arduinos in the past, but this was my first introduction to Raspberry Pi. Although Pis contain a full OS and may be used as a desktop, they are similar to Arduinos in some ways. (click on the picture to learn more)

We designed a traffic light controller which is shown in the accompanying video. Using the Raspberry Pi for logic and control, the two LEDs act as stop lights. The push button acts as a button for pedestrians to press when crossing the road. Then, the countdown provides them time to cross the road and then the light turns back to red.

This was a great introduction to Raspberry Pis and it invigorated me to experiment and tinker with them more.
